## Design Context

### Users
English-speaking visitors exploring NASA's exoplanet archive, plus GitHub users evaluating an open-source demo. They want a calm, visually rich way to discover interesting worlds, filter quickly, compare planets, and understand the archive without spreadsheet friction.

### Brand Personality
Curious, cinematic, precise. The product should feel trustworthy like a scientific instrument, but welcoming like a well-edited magazine. The emotional goal is confidence plus delight, not hype.

### Aesthetic Direction
NASA-inspired deep-space with glassmorphism, strong contrast, and restrained motion. The site should feel modern, premium, and slightly editorial, with a clear hierarchy, bento-style rhythm where useful, and a dark/light system that stays cohesive in both modes.

### Design Principles
- Make the featured worlds and explorer the visual center of gravity.
- Break repetition with asymmetry, varied card heights, and stronger section hierarchy.
- Keep spacing breathable so dense data still feels calm and easy to scan.
- Prefer purposeful motion, hover feedback, and micro-interactions over decoration.
- Preserve fast, static-site performance and clean, readable content structure.
